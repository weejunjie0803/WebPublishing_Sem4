NASI LEMAK - Malays' Cuisine

Story

Coconut rice, chili on the side, slivers of anchovy, nuts, and a boiled egg: meet Malaysia’s national dish, the nasi lemak. A simple everyday meal gracing the dinner table of Malaysians all across the country, this beloved dish has a history as humble as its ingredients. Nasi lemak, being a cheap and loved pre-packaged meal, is sold almost everywhere, from little roadside stalls to five-star restaurants that provide buffets and fusion foods.

AIS KACANG MACHINE - "Dessert"

The ais kacang is prepared by putting red beans, cendol and sweet corn inside a bowl. Placed the bowl under this ice shaved machine. Then, by placing a thawed block ice inside ice holder. Turn on the machine and ice is then grated into ice droplet. Drizzled rose syrup and palm sugar on top of the ice. As a final touch, adding an evaporat milk to it. Some may add an ice cream as top of the ais kacang.

FRY PASTRY STALL - Breakfast

Story

Youtiao, which twists two pieces of dough joined in the middle and then deep-fries in hot oil, was created by the people to curse Qin and his wife sur-named Wang in Southern Song Dynasty. Youtiao is a popular breakfast food and perfect companion with soy milk. It is one of the “big 4s” in the Shanghai-style breakfast, together with soy milk, big flatbread and glutinous rice rolls.
